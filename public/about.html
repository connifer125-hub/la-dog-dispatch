Our Story

Built by someone who got tired of watching the system fail.

No matter how much she donated,

The frustration

A 20-year tech executive who kept asking: why is this still manual?

LA Dog Dispatch was built by someone who spent years donating, sharing, and watching. She understood technology. She understood systems. And what she saw in LA's shelter ecosystem made no sense to her.

City shelter systems were not deploying modern automation or coordination tools ‚Äî leaving urgent dogs trapped in a fragmented, manual flow that was costing lives every single day.

"I kept donating and dogs kept dying. I finally realized the problem wasn't funding ‚Äî it was that the right people couldn't find each other fast enough."

The broken chain

The system wasn't failing for lack of heart. It was failing for lack of coordination.

Donors pledge in comment threads ‚Äî but rescues can't act on informal promises. Money that exists never reaches the dog.
Rescues hesitate without confirmed funding. They want to pull ‚Äî but saying yes without a foster or a dollar is a gamble they can't always take.
Fosters don't know where to plug in. There's no centralized place to say "I'm available" and actually get connected to a rescue that needs them.
The clock often starts too late. By the time a dog's listing reaches the right people, days of their window are already gone.

The community couldn't keep waiting for the government to modernize the system. So she built it herself.

The solution

We built the coordination layer
the shelter system never had.

LA Dog Dispatch automatically creates a funding page the moment a dog is euth-listed ‚Äî activating donors immediately, alerting the foster network, and giving rescues the confirmed resources they need to say yes.

When the system moves faster, more dogs get a real chance. We hope you'll join this network ‚Äî or donate to help us save more.
