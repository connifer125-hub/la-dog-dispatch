Our Story

Why LA Dog Dispatch Exists

The Frustration

LA Dog Dispatch was built by a 20-year tech executive who realized something frustrating:

No matter how many people care, dogs are still dying.

Not because rescues aren't working hard. Not because donors don't care.

The people are there. The community is there. The rescues are there. But the technology connecting them is too slow.

In a world where information travels in seconds, urgent dogs are still relying on manual posts, buried shelter listings, and scattered outreach.

The clock moves fast. The visibility doesn't.

So we built the automation to give every urgent dog immediate visibility — the moment they're listed.

The Broken Chain

The system wasn't failing for lack of heart. It was failing for lack of coordination.

Sharers want to help but don't know which dogs are urgent or how to create a good post. By the time they find out, it's often too late.

Rescues hesitate without confirmed fosters. They want to pull — but saying yes without a foster lined up is a gamble they can't always take.

Fosters don't know where to plug in. There's no centralized place to say "I'm available" and get connected to a rescue that needs them.

The clock often starts too late. By the time a listing reaches the right people, days of the window are already gone.

We couldn't keep waiting for the government to modernize the system. So we built it.

The Solution

LA Dog Dispatch automates visibility for every urgent dog.

The moment a dog is euth-listed, LA Dog Dispatch automatically creates a visibility page — with a pre-built share card, caption, and rescue tags ready to go. The community can act immediately instead of finding out too late.

We're building toward per-dog funding so rescues have real dollars behind every pull. For now, every share is a lifeline. We hope you'll join this network.
